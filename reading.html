Notes on the Synthesis of Form
Christopher Alexander

June 2011 - June 2011

I got interested in this book after watching Ryan Singer's talk about it. I decided to read it after noticing that almost all of the well known OO design thinkers either recommend it or mention it as one of their inspirations.

This is a book about the process of design. About finding what you need to design an how it fits within it's "context". Most examples are from architecture, urbanism and industrial design but this book really is about design in general. It is not hard to see how you could apply parts of this process to the design of your software. It was especially interesting for me to see how the "unsefconscious process", now impractical for designing buildings, is like the agile processes we use.

Test Driven Development: By Example
Kent Beck

May 2011 - June 2011

This is a nice book for people who would like an introduction to TDD. That said, I thought that it could have been replaced by two screencasts and a book of about 50 pages. It has a lot of "filler" material which is a bit annoying. I think I expected a bit more but overall it\'s an interesting read.

Eloquent Ruby
Russ Olsen

April 2011 - May 2011

Awesome book about how Ruby is actually used and how to avoid "programming Java in Ruby". It is somewhat similar to Effective Java. It took me a while to read it but it is a quick read. The perfect second Ruby book.
