Reading List

Refactoring
Martin Fowler, Kent Beck, John Brant, William Opdyke and Don Roberts

Started in August 2011

Apprenticeship Patterns
Dave H. Hoover and Adewale Oshineye

August 2011

Enjoyed reading this one.

The RSpec Book
David Chelimsky, Dave Astels, Bryan Helmkamp, Dan North, Zach Dennis and Aslak Heliesoy

June - August 2011

I've heard about this book but never really considered reading it because I thought that I don't need a book about one testing tool.

It turns out that this book should have been named "The BDD Book" because It's more about the BDD workflow than it is about RSpec. That said, It does go into significant detail with RSpec and I liked that part the most.

Ruby on Rails 3 Tutorial: Learn Rails by Example
Michael Hartl

June - July 2011

I really liked this Rails tutorial-book because it drives the sample application with RSpec.

Notes on the Synthesis of Form
Christopher Alexander

June 2011

I got interested in this book after watching Ryan Singer's talk about it. I decided to read it after noticing that almost all of the well known OO design thinkers either recommend it or mention it as one of their inspirations.

This is a book about the process of design. About finding what you need to design and how it fits within it's "context". Most examples are from architecture, urbanism and industrial design but this book really is about design in general. It's not hard to see how you could apply parts of this process to the design of your software.

Test Driven Development: By Example
Kent Beck

May - June 2011

This is a nice book for people who would like an introduction to TDD. That said, I thought that it could have been replaced by two screencasts and a book of about 50 pages. It has a lot of "filler" material which is a bit annoying. I think I expected a bit more but overall it's an interesting read.

Eloquent Ruby
Russ Olsen

April - May 2011

Awesome book about how Ruby is actually used and how to avoid "programming Java in Ruby". It is somewhat similar to Effective Java. It took me a while to read it but it is a quick read. The perfect second Ruby book.

Agile Web Development with Rails
Sam Ruby, Dave Thomas and David Heinemeier Hansson
